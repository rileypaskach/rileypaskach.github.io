What I Do

My job is to help connect people to the online audience they are trying to reach. The internet is a competitive landscape, and many people have a ton to offer the world if they could just gain enough traction to get started. There is nothing more gratifying than being able to bridge that gap and bring people the traffic they need. I have worked on paid and organic campaigns for clients of all types, and I have found that the process outlined below will reveal the leaky spots in any conversion funnel.

Identify Goals and Investigate Problems

No matter the client, the first step will be to define what a "Conversion" looks like for them. Someone new to digital marketing might have no idea, but for experienced clients we may need to start off discussing what their online conversions look like in the real world, and how we can make adjustments to improve results. My next immediate step is to deep-dive into their Analytics. Conversion totals are the end of a long journey, and it's important to determine if anyone is getting lost along the way

Test Solutions

This part is a bit more difficult to outline, even thematically. The variation of goals and current performance will mean that this part could be almost anything. SEO is always part of the solution because you should be working on your organic ranking at all times. If budget allows, there is always a Paid Search strategy that will help someone reach their goals.

Build on Momentum

By analyzing site traffic data, I'll gain new insights that I'll use to further refine the overall marketing strategy. Each new iteration is an opportunity to learn more and improve on our digital marketing efforts. I will review Heatmaps and A/B tested pages, which are incredibly useful tools for CRO. I do things this way because I am able to avoid wasting both data and time; improvements ripple outward and feed into more improvements.
